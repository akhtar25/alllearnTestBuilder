Surviving with Myeloma

It's disheartening to find out when you or your loved one is diagnosed with cancer. We cannot take away from the seriousness of the situation. But it's very important to understand that this is a challenge that life has presented in front of you. There's only one way to get on the other side, and that's through it. Every day there are new developements in the field of myeloma medication that improve the longevity and the quality of life of the patients. Most of these treatments are available in India soon after they've been approved and tested thoroughly. All of the major hospitals in our country boast of the latest treament methodologies. However, it's important to understand that since every person's age, health and stage is different the treatment is chosen accordingly by the doctors. It's also important to know that our country has one of the best hospitals and doctors in this field. Hospitals like AIIMS, Delhi have one of the best teams of oncologists and experts of Multiple Myeloma so you need not worry about looking for too many places.

What is Myeloma?

Cancer starts when cells begin to grow out of control. Cells in nearly any part of the body can become cancer, and can spread to other areas. Multiple myeloma is a cancer of plasma cells. Normal plasma cells are found in the bone marrow and are an important part of the immune system.The immune system is made up of several types of cells that work together to fight infections and other diseases. Lymphocytes are one of the main types of white blood cells in the immune system and include T cells and B cells. When B cells respond to an infection, they mature and change into plasma cells. Plasma cells make the antibodies (also called immunoglobulins) that help the body attack and kill germs. Plasma cells, are found mainly in the bone marrow. In general, when plasma cells become cancerous and grow out of control, this is called multiple myeloma.
